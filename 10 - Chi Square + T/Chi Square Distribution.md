Chi-squared distribution comes from summing the squares of standard normal RVs.

If $Z \sim N(0,1)$, $Z^2$ follows a chi squared distribution with one degree of freedom $Z \sim \chi^2_{(1)}$

If $Z_1, ... ,Z_k \sim$ iid $N(0,1)$, then $Z_1^2 + ... + Z_k^2 \sim \chi^2_{(k)}$
